## Goal

Implement the invoicing system that generates, stores, and delivers invoices for subscription charges and metered usage. Invoices are generated through Stripe and synchronized locally for display and archival.

---

## Business Context

Invoices are legal and financial documents. Every subscription payment and metered charge must be documented with a proper invoice. Customers need to view, download, and pay invoices. The invoicing system must handle monthly recurring charges, one-time charges, credits, refunds, and adjustments. Invoice data must be immutable and retained for the legally required period.

---

## Technical Context

Invoices are generated by Stripe's billing system. The platform syncs invoice data locally by processing Stripe webhook events (`invoice.paid`, `invoice.payment_failed`, `invoice.created`). Local invoice records cache the invoice data for fast display in the billing portal. Invoice PDFs are stored in Cloudflare R2 for download. The invoicing service handles: invoice synchronization, credit note processing, refund documentation, and invoice display data preparation.

## Requirements

### Functional requirements

- Sync invoices from Stripe: store invoice number, amount, currency, status (paid, open, void, uncollectible), period start/end, paid date, line items, and PDF URL
- Generate invoice PDFs via Stripe and store a copy in R2 for redundancy
- Display invoice list in billing portal with: invoice number, date, period, amount, status, download link
- Handle credit notes: sync credit note data from Stripe, display as negative line items
- Handle refunds: document refund transactions linked to original invoice
- Support invoice line items breakdown: subscription (pro-rated), metered usage, add-ons, credits, taxes
- Provide invoice search by number, date range, status, and amount range
- Generate invoice summary: total billed this year, average monthly charge, payment history
- Handle multi-currency invoices (USD primary, support additional currencies)
- Implement invoice archival: mark old invoices as archived after 3 years

### Non-functional requirements

- Invoice data is immutable after synchronization
- Invoice PDFs are cached in R2 with CDN delivery
- Invoice list queries must use local data (not Stripe API) for performance
- Invoice sync must be complete within 30 seconds of Stripe event

### Performance requirements

- Invoice list load: < 1s for 100 invoices
- Invoice PDF download: < 2s (CDN-cached)
- Invoice search: < 500ms
- Invoice sync from Stripe: < 3s per invoice

### Security requirements

- Invoice data is workspace-scoped — a workspace can only see its own invoices
- Invoice PDFs must use signed URLs with 1-hour expiration
- Invoice numbers are sequential but not guessable
- Tax information must be handled according to local regulations

### Accessibility requirements

- Invoice tables must be keyboard navigable
- Invoice status must have text labels and color indicators
- Download links must have accessible labels

## Architecture Constraints

- Invoice syncing must be idempotent — the same invoice webhook can be processed multiple times
- Invoice PDFs are fetched from Stripe once and stored in R2 — subsequent requests use the R2 copy
- Local invoice records are the source of truth for the billing portal — Stripe is the source of truth for financial records
- Line items are stored as an array within the invoice document (not a separate collection) for query simplicity
- Credit notes are linked to invoices via `invoiceId` reference

## Notes

Invoice PDFs are stored at `r2://invoices/{workspaceId}/{invoiceNumber}.pdf`. The Stripe invoice PDF URL has a limited lifetime — always fetch and cache the PDF immediately when the `invoice.created` or `invoice.paid` event arrives. For tax compliance, store the raw tax breakdown from Stripe's invoice line items. Consider implementing an invoice email delivery system that sends the invoice PDF as an attachment.
