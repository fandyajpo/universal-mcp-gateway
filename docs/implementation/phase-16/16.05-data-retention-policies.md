## Goal

Implement configurable data retention policies that allow enterprise workspaces to define how long different types of data are retained, with automated purging, legal hold capabilities, and compliance reporting.

---

## Business Context

Enterprise customers have legal and regulatory requirements for data retention. Different data types have different retention requirements: audit logs must be retained for 1-7 years depending on jurisdiction, chat history may be retained for 90 days, deleted documents may have a 30-day soft-delete window. Enterprise admins need the ability to configure retention periods, place legal holds on specific data, and audit the retention enforcement.

---

## Technical Context

Data retention policies are stored per workspace in a `retention_policies` collection. Each policy applies to a data type (audit_log, chat_message, document, user_session, api_key, etc.) and specifies the retention period and the action to take when the period expires (delete, archive, anonymize). A scheduled Inngest job runs daily to enforce retention policies. Legal hold overrides retention — data under legal hold is never purged.

## Requirements

### Functional requirements

- Define retention policy per data type: audit_log, chat_message, document, user_session, api_key, usage_event, webhook_event, notification, connector_data
- Each policy specifies: data type, retention period (days), action on expiry (delete, archive, anonymize), priority (standard, compliance, custom)
- Default policies are created for all data types with sensible defaults
- Enterprise workspace admins can configure custom retention periods within allowed ranges
- Legal hold: place legal hold on specific data (by user, workspace, document, or date range) — data under hold is never purged
- Legal hold tracking: reason, date placed, date lifted, authorized by
- Automated purging: daily Inngest cron enforces retention policies across all data types
- Archive action: move data to long-term cold storage (R2 Glacier) before deletion
- Anonymize action: replace PII fields with anonymized values while retaining structural data
- Retention audit log: record all purge operations for compliance
- Admin retention dashboard: view current policies, purge history, active legal holds

### Non-functional requirements

- Retention enforcement must process data in batches (1000 records per batch)
- Policy configuration changes must not affect data that has already been purged
- Legal hold must be immediately effective (prevent purge of held data)
- Archive storage must be cost-effective (cold storage tier)

### Performance requirements

- Daily purge processing: must complete within 2 hours for 100M records
- Per-type purge: < 30 minutes for largest collection (audit_logs)
- Policy lookup: < 20ms (cached)
- Legal hold check: < 50ms per purge candidate

### Security requirements

- Purged data must be irrecoverable after the retention period (secure deletion)
- Archived data must be encrypted at rest in cold storage
- Legal hold must require `admin:compliance` permission to place or lift
- Anonymized data must not be reversible
- Retention policy changes must be logged to audit trail

### Accessibility requirements

- Retention policy tables must be keyboard accessible

---

## Architecture Constraints

- Retention policies are workspace-scoped — each workspace configures its own policies
- Default policies apply to all workspaces unless overridden
- Purge engine processes one data type at a time to avoid long-running transactions
- Legal hold is checked before any purge — data under hold is skipped with a log entry
- Archive action moves data to `r2://archived/{workspaceId}/{dataType}/{date}/{id}.json.gzip` with server-side encryption
- Anonymize action replaces: name → `[redacted]`, email → hash, IP → `0.0.0.0`, user agent → `[redacted]`

## Notes

Data retention is a legal compliance feature. The default retention periods should be reviewed by legal counsel before deployment. Recommended defaults: audit_logs = 365 days, chat_messages = 90 days, documents (soft-delete) = 30 days, usage_events = 90 days, user_sessions = 30 days after expiry, api_keys = 90 days after deletion. Legal hold should be a rare event triggered by actual legal proceedings — the UI should make this clear.
